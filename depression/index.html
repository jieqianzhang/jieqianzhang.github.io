Visa

H1B visa is required for international students to work legally in the US, upon acceptance of a major-related work offer, sponsorship from the company and successful lottery selection. In 2015, 233,000 H1B petitions were filed for the 65,000 spots.

Culture

Most Chinese social values are derived from Confucianism and Taoism. The cultural values influence its national psychology and identity. Confucianism and Taoism have an emphasis on courtesy, which stresses modesty and prudence. It is about respecting laws and preventing misconduct. As a result, many Chinese students appear not willing to take ricks and seize opportunities.

Marriage

In 2013, the average age of first marriage in the US is 27 for women and 29 for men, as opposed to 24 and 26 in China. Among the 200,000 Chinese students to begin their study in the US every year, around 46% are around such age, pursuing advanced degrees. It is recognized in Chinese culture that marriage bonds not only the couple, but also entire social connections behind the two families.

Finance

US median annual household income was $52,000 in 2013. China’s was around $20,000. The annual costs of studying in the US, take UC Berkeley for example, are around $33,000 for in-state students, $60,000 for international students.

Language

Most of the Chinese students have to pass either the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) or The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) to study in the United States. However, passing the exam doesn’t necessary mean Chinese students are completely fluent in English and can fully understand the cultural, historical and academic references behind the language. Many of them still found themselves struggling in classes after passing the language exam.

Education

Education in China focuses a lot on cramming information into students’ heads. Teachers often evaluate one’s academic performances solely based on his/her ability to memorize textbooks. However, in United States, students are encouraged to think critically and ask questions. Many Chinese students have a hard time adapting two completely teaching styles.
